Gnoccis

Gnocchi are a type of Italian dumpling made with potatoes, flour, and eggs. They have a soft and pillowy texture and are typically served with a sauce or topped with cheese or herbs. Gnocchi can be shaped by hand or with a fork to create ridges that help hold onto the sauce. They can be made with just a few simple ingredients and can be boiled in minutes, making them a quick and easy meal option. Overall, gnocchi are a classic Italian dish that are both delicious and versatile.
Ingredients
  • 2 lbs russet potatoes
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • Salt, to taste
  • Optional: grated Parmesan cheese or chopped fresh herbs for serving
  1. Boil the potatoes in salted water until they are tender, about 20 minutes.
  2. Drain the potatoes and let them cool until they can be handled.
  3. Peel the potatoes and pass them through a potato ricer or a fine-mesh sieve onto a floured work surface.
  4. Make a well in the center of the potatoes and add the flour and egg. Season with salt to taste.
  5. Knead the ingredients together until a smooth dough forms. You can add more flour if the dough is too sticky.
  6. Divide the dough into 4 equal portions. Roll each portion into a long rope about 1 inch thick. Cut the rope into 1-inch pieces.
  7. To shape the gnocchi, use a fork or your thumb to make a small indentation on each piece and press down gently to create a ridged surface.
  8. Boil the gnocchi in salted water until they float to the surface, about 2-3 minutes.
  9. Serve the gnocchi with your favorite sauce, grated parmesan cheese, or chopped fresh herbs.
