Pasta

Pasta is a staple food made from durum wheat flour and water. It is an important component of many cuisines around the world, particularly in Italy where it has been a staple food for centuries. Pasta comes in a variety of shapes, sizes and textures, including spaghetti, penne, fettuccine, and more. It is typically boiled in water until it is cooked, and then served with a sauce or other ingredients. Pasta is a versatile food that can be enjoyed in a variety of dishes and is widely considered to be a comfort food for many people.
Ingredients
  • 1 pound of durum wheat flour
  • Water
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 tablespoon of olive oil
  • Herbs and spices of your choice (optional)
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, eggs, salt, and olive oil. Mix until the ingredients are well incorporated.
  2. Knead the dough by hand on a floured surface until it is smooth and elastic. This should take about 10 minutes. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour. If it is too dry, add a little more water.
  3. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. After 30 minutes, roll the dough out into thin sheets, using a rolling pin or a pasta machine. Cut the pasta into the desired shape (such as spaghetti, fettuccine, or penne).
  5. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until it is al dente. Drain the pasta and set it aside.
  6. In a separate pan, heat your choice of sauce over medium heat. Add any herbs or spices, if desired.
  7. Toss the cooked pasta in the sauce and serve immediately, garnished with grated cheese and additional herbs, if desired.
